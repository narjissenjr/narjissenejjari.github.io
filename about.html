About

Bio

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the National School of Computer Science and Systems Analysis (ENSIAS), Rabat and the International University of Rabat (UIR), specializing in AI-driven cybersecurity analytics. My research focuses on AI-enabled methods for cyber threat detection, statistical modeling, and causal inference, aiming to improve decision-making under uncertainty in complex information systems.

I have extensive experience in teaching and mentoring, delivering courses in cybersecurity, programming, and machine learning, and supervising end-of-year student projects across multiple institutions.

Research Interests

  • AI-enabled Cybersecurity Analytics: Automatic cyber threat detection, cyber threat analysis, and privacy-preserving systems
  • Statistical and Machine Learning Methodology
  • Causal Explanation vs. Predictive Analytics: Balancing explanatory power with predictive performance
  • Applications in Information Systems and Cybersecurity

Experience

Before completing my Ph.D., I conducted research on data breaches, cybercrime analysis, and predictive modeling in various academic projects, collaborating with interdisciplinary teams. I have also contributed to research grant projects funded by NATO and presented at international conferences such as NETYS, SNAMS, and AIxIA.

Education

  • Master of Data Science and Big Data, ENSIAS, Mohammed V University, Rabat (October 2018)
  • Bachelor in Mathematics and Computer Science, Mohammed Ist University, Oujda (December 2016)

Outside of Work

I enjoy applying computational and statistical methods to real-world problems in cybersecurity, while mentoring young researchers and students interested in AI and security analytics.
